This submission formalizes the main proof of Bonnet, Feghali, Nguyen, Scott,
Seymour, Thomasse, and Trotignon, \emph{Graphs without a 3-Connected Subgraph
are 4-Colourable}.  It records the definitions of three-connected subgraphs,
\(m\)-fragile graphs, and the four coloring-extension conditions of Theorem 3.
It also supplies proof evidence for Theorem 3, the contrapositive and
chromatic-number forms of Theorem 2, and the corollary that every finite simple
graph with no three-connected subgraph is four-colorable.
