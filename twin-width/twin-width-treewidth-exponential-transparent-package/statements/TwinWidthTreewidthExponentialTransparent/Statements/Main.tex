\paragraph{Statement.}
For every natural number \(k\), there is a finite type \(V\), decidable equality
on \(V\), a simple graph \(G\) on \(V\), and a tree decomposition \(D\) of \(G\)
such that
\[
  \Bigl(\max_i |D_i|\Bigr)-1 \le 2k+4 .
\]
In addition, \(G\) has no contraction sequence whose red degree is bounded by
\(2^k\).  The Lean statement says this directly as
\[
  \neg\,\mathrm{Nonempty}
    (\mathrm{ContractionSequence}\ G\ (2^k)).
\]
Thus the submitted entry does not introduce or submit aliases for graph
parameters, treewidth, or twin-width.  The treewidth side is witnessed by the
explicit decomposition \(D\), and the twin-width side is phrased as the
nonexistence of a small contraction sequence.

\paragraph{Proof content.}
The proof instantiates \(G\) with the explicit Bonnet--Depres graph
\(BD_k\) and \(D\) with its explicit Bonnet--Depres tree decomposition.  The
submitted proof uses the proved bound on the maximum bag size of this
decomposition and the proved theorem that no \(2^k\)-bounded contraction
sequence exists for \(BD_k\).  No contract axiom supplies the result: both
facts are proved from the Bonnet--Depres construction in the vendored source
files.
