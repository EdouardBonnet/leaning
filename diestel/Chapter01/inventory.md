## Numbered Statements

- Proposition 1.2.1: handshaking lemma, odd-degree vertices are even.
- Proposition 1.2.2: subgraph with minimum degree above its edge/vertex ratio.
- Proposition 1.3.1: long path and cycle from minimum degree.
- Proposition 1.3.2: `g(G) ≤ 2 * diam(G) + 1`.
- Proposition 1.3.3: radius/maximum-degree upper bound on order.
- Theorem 1.3.4: Alon-Hoory-Linial Moore bound using average degree.
- Corollary 1.3.5: minimum degree at least `3` implies `g(G) < 2 log |G|`.
- Proposition 1.4.1: connected graphs admit connected vertex enumerations.
- Proposition 1.4.2: `κ(G) ≤ λ(G) ≤ δ(G)` for non-trivial graphs.
- Theorem 1.4.3: Mader's average-degree forcing theorem.
- Theorem 1.5.1: equivalent characterizations of trees.
- Corollary 1.5.2: connected graph with `n` vertices is a tree iff it has `n-1`
  edges.
- Corollary 1.5.3: every graph of minimum degree greater than `|T|-1` contains `T`.
- Lemma 1.5.4: separation properties of normal trees.
- Proposition 1.5.5: every connected graph has a normal spanning tree.
- Proposition 1.6.1: bipartite iff no odd cycle.
- Proposition 1.7.1: minor and topological-minor relations are partial orders
  on finite graphs up to isomorphism.
- Corollary 1.7.2: finite minors are obtained by edge deletion, contraction and
  vertex deletion.
- Proposition 1.7.3: topological minor implies minor; converse for maximum
  degree at most `3`.
- Theorem 1.8.1: Euler's theorem for connected graphs.
- Proposition 1.9.1: cycle-space characterizations.
- Proposition 1.9.2: cuts plus empty set form the cut space generated by atomic
  cuts.
- Lemma 1.9.3: every cut is a disjoint union of bonds.
- Theorem 1.9.4: cycle space and cut space are orthogonal complements.
- Theorem 1.9.5: fundamental cycles and cuts form bases; dimensions. The
  formal cycle-space dimension is written `m + 1 - n`, not `m - n + 1`,
  because Lean's natural-number subtraction is truncated.
- Proposition 1.9.6: kernel/image of the incidence matrix.
- Proposition 1.9.7: `B * Bᵀ = A + D`.

## Proof pass status

Finalized proof files:

- `proposition_1_2_1.lean`: follows from Mathlib's handshaking lemma.
- `proposition_1_2_2.lean`: finite extremal induced-subgraph argument, maximizing
  edge/vertex ratio and then minimizing the vertex count among maximizers.
- `proposition_1_3_1.lean`: uses Mathlib's maximal path theorem, endpoint-neighbour
  counting, and the standard farthest-neighbour cycle construction.
- `proposition_1_3_2.lean`: shortest-cycle proof. If `g(G) > 2 * diam(G) + 1`, an
  arc of length `diam(G) + 1` on a shortest cycle can be replaced by a shortest path
  of length at most `diam(G)`. A local path-union subgraph lemma turns the two
  unequal paths into a shorter cycle, contradicting minimality of the girth.
- `proposition_1_3_3.lean`: finite breadth-first layer count around a centre. The proof uses
  Mathlib's radius/eccentricity API, bounds the first layer by maximum degree, bounds later layers
  by the non-parent neighbour choices, and closes with the geometric sum estimate.
- `proposition_1_4_1.lean`: finite induction using Mathlib's removable-vertex
  theorem for connected finite nontrivial graphs.
- `proposition_1_4_2.lean`: proves `κ(G) ≤ λ(G) ≤ δ(G)`. The proof shows
  finite vertex-connectivity implies the corresponding Mathlib edge-connectivity
  by induction on `k`: after deleting fewer than `k` vertices, deleting one edge
  preserves connectedness because the induced graph remains connected after
  deleting either endpoint. The upper bound uses
  `SimpleGraph.IsEdgeConnected.le_degree`.
- `theorem_1_4_3.lean`: proves Mader's theorem. The proof follows Diestel's
  smallest dense subgraph argument: choose a smallest induced subgraph satisfying
  `‖H‖ > ε(G)(|H|-k)`, prove no such subgraph has order exactly `2k`, derive
  `δ(H)>ε(G)`, and show any proper separation of order at most `k` would split
  the edges into two smaller non-candidates whose edge bounds contradict the
  density of `H`.
- `theorem_1_5_1.lean`: follows from Mathlib's tree characterizations.
- `corollary_1_5_2.lean`: follows from Mathlib's tree edge-count theorem.
- `corollary_1_5_3.lean`: finite induction on the tree; delete a leaf using
  Mathlib's leaf-removal connectedness lemma, embed the smaller induced tree,
  and extend the copy to the leaf using the strict minimum-degree bound.
- `proposition_1_6_1.lean`: reduces the statement to Mathlib's closed-walk
  characterization of two-colourability, with a local strong-induction proof
  that every odd closed walk contains an odd cycle.
- `corollary_1_7_2.lean`: proves the current finite-branch-set model contract.
- `proposition_1_7_1.lean`: proves reflexivity, transitivity and finite
  antisymmetry for the custom ordinary-minor and topological-minor relations.
  The topological transitivity proof composes models by substituting each
  middle-model edge path into each source-model edge path, then bypasses repeats.
  The key support lemma proves distinct source edge paths in a topological model
  have disjoint ambient edge sets, so composed internal vertices cannot collide.
- `theorem_1_8_1.lean`: Euler's theorem. The proof takes a longest trail, uses
  the even-degree hypothesis to show it is closed, rotates it to rule out unused
  edges incident with its support, and then uses connectedness to show every edge
  is used.
- `proposition_1_9_2.lean`: finite-graph cut-space characterization. The contract now carries
  the finite vertex hypothesis implicit in Section 1.9; the proof shows cuts/empty-set generate
  `cut_space`, then proves every cut vector is the mod-2 sum of its atomic vertex cuts.
- `lemma_1_9_3.lean`: finite induction on the size of a cut. If a non-empty cut is not
  already a bond, choose a smaller non-empty cut inside it; the difference is again a cut
  because symmetric difference of cut sides realizes symmetric difference of cuts. The two
  smaller decompositions combine disjointly.
- `cut_space_incidence_image.lean`: supporting lemma for `1.9.6`; rewrites `cut_space` as the
  image of the mod-2 vertex-edge incidence map, using `proposition_1_9_2`.
- `cycle_space_list_repr.lean`: supporting lemmas for `1.9.1` and `1.9.6`; rewrites
  membership in `cycle_space` as a finite sum of cycle vectors via span induction,
  proves cycle-vector incidence parity, and proves the linear forward implication
  from `cycle_space` membership to zero mod-2 incidence sums.
- `cycle_space_even_support.lean`: supporting lemmas for the converse direction of
  `1.9.1`; defines the support graph of an edge-space vector locally and proves its
  vertex degrees are exactly the incidence counts of selected edges. It also proves
  that a nonzero even-incidence support graph contains a cycle, and that such a
  support-graph cycle transfers to a cycle in `G` all of whose edges are selected.
- `proposition_1_9_1.lean`: proves both cycle-space characterizations. The first is the
  finite-list form of span membership. The second proves the forward parity implication
  linearly from cycle vectors, and the converse by induction on the number of selected
  edges: a nonzero even-incidence support graph contains a selected cycle, which can be
  deleted to reduce the support.
- `proposition_1_9_6.lean`: combines the parity characterization from `1.9.1` with
  `cut_space_incidence_image.lean` to identify the incidence-matrix kernel with the
  cycle space and the transpose image with the cut space.
- `theorem_1_9_4.lean`: proves the orthogonal-complement theorem. The first inclusion
  is the direct incidence pairing calculation between cycle vectors and cut vectors.
  The reverse cut-orthogonal inclusion tests against single-vertex incidence vectors,
  while the reverse cycle-orthogonal inclusion uses Mathlib's finite-dimensional
  `range_dualMap_eq_dualAnnihilator_ker` theorem for the incidence map over `ZMod 2`.
- `cut_cycle_space_dimension.lean`: supporting dimension lemmas for `1.9.5`. It proves
  that for connected finite graphs, the cut space has dimension `n - 1` and the cycle
  space has dimension `m + 1 - n`, using the incidence map, its transpose, and
  finite-dimensional rank-nullity over `ZMod 2`.
- `theorem_1_9_5.lean`: proves the fundamental-cycle and fundamental-cut existence
  statements, plus the dimension formulas. The cycle proof adds a chord to the unique
  tree path between its endpoints. The cut proof takes the reachability side after
  deleting a tree edge, decomposes the resulting cut into bonds using `1.9.3`, and
  selects the bond containing the deleted tree edge.
- `proposition_1_9_7.lean`: follows from Mathlib's incidence-matrix identity.

  including transitivity, antisymmetry, chain comparability on root paths,
  generic first/last walk-crossing lemmas, separator support, and maximal-component
  support lemmas for down-closed deletions.
- `lemma_1_5_4.lean`: proves both clauses of Diestel's normal-tree lemma:
  incomparable vertices are separated by the intersection of their down-closures,
  and maximal connected components outside a down-closed set are exactly the
  up-closure of a minimal remaining vertex. `#print axioms` reports only standard
  Lean/Classical axioms (`propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`), with no
  project contract axiom.
- `proposition_1_7_1_support.lean`: packages the `1.7.1` clauses other than
  topological-minor transitivity; `proposition_1_7_1.lean` adds that final clause.
- `average_degree.lean`: proves reusable arithmetic for Diestel's rational
  average degree and edge/vertex ratio: `d(G) = 2ε(G)`, nonnegativity,
  positivity implies nonempty vertex type, and `d(G) < |G|` for nonempty
  finite simple graphs.
- `moore_bound.lean`: begins support for the Section 1.3 Moore-bound arguments.
  It proves that an `egirth ≥ g` hypothesis forces every cycle to have length
  at least `g`, that two distinct simple paths with common endpoints force
  `g ≤ |P|+|Q|`, and consequently that two simple paths whose length sum is
  below `g` are equal. It also defines fixed-length rooted paths and proves
  their endpoint map is injective below half the girth, the first cardinality
  ingredient for the breadth-first tree-counting arguments. It now also proves
  the minimum-degree `δ ≥ 3` forward-extension step for non-backtracking paths,
  injectivity of that extension below half the girth, the recursive count
  `2 * |P_n| ≤ |P_{n+1}|`, and the rooted lower bound
  `2^n ≤ |V(G)|` whenever `2n < g`. For the logarithmic corollary it also
  proves the sharper rooted count `3·2^(n-1) ≤ |V(G)|` and the even-girth
  edge-root count `2·2^n < |V(G)|`, using two non-backtracking path trees
  growing from opposite ends of an edge. It now also includes the variable-layer
  endpoint injections for rooted paths of length at most `r` and for both
  non-backtracking trees rooted at the ends of one edge; these are the
	  upper-bound side needed for the full `n₀(d,g)` Moore expression. The
	  forward-extension step has also been generalized from branching factor `2`
	  to branching factor `d - 1`, giving the exact-layer lower bound
	  `d·(d-1)^(n-1) ≤ |V(G)|` under `δ(G) ≥ d`, `d ≥ 2`, and `2n < g`.
	  The rooted odd-radius layers have now been summed, proving
	  `1 + ∑_{i<r} d·(d-1)^i ≤ |V(G)|` under `δ(G) ≥ d`, `d ≥ 2`, and
	  `2r < g`. For the full Alon-Hoory-Linial theorem, it now also defines
	  `IsNonreturning`, proves that every non-returning walk of length below the
	  girth is a path, packages fixed-length non-returning walks, proves their
	  endpoint map is injective below half the girth, and identifies global
	  length-one non-returning walks with oriented darts, giving cardinality
	  `∑ v, d(v)`. It also formalizes admissible one-step extensions of
	  non-returning walks, proves that the global extension map is injective
	  into the next layer, and expresses the extension-domain cardinality as a
	  Sigma sum over the available continuations of each current walk. For
	  positive layers it proves each walk ending at `x` has at least
	  `d(x)-1` admissible continuations, hence
	  `∑_W (d(end W)-1) ≤ |W_{n+1}|`.
	  After adding `Chapter01/ahl.pdf` as the source for the full
	  Alon-Hoory-Linial proof, it also packages global non-returning walks of
	  length at most `r` and proves their endpoint-pair map is injective when
	  `2r < g`, giving the odd-girth AHL upper-bound side
	  `|W_{\le r}| ≤ |V(G)|^2`.
- `corollary_1_3_5.lean`: proves the minimum-degree logarithmic girth bound.
  The proof uses the sharper minimum-degree rooted counts directly: if `g` is
  odd, it applies the rooted count at radius `g/2`; if `g` is even, it applies
  the strict edge-root count at radius `g/2 - 1`, then converts the resulting
  powers of two to Diestel's base-two logarithm inequality.
- `mader.lean`: develops support for Theorem 1.4.3. It defines Diestel's dense
  candidate subgraphs `‖G[U]‖ > ε(G)(|U|-k)`, proves the whole graph is a
  candidate under `d(G)>4k`, selects a smallest candidate, excludes order
  exactly `2k`, proves deletion of a low-degree vertex preserves candidacy,
  derives `δ(H)>ε(G)`, `|H|>k+1`, proves the required deleted-vertex
  connectedness, and proves the average-degree conclusions `d(H)>d(G)-2k>2k`
  for a smallest candidate.

- `1.3.4`: proved in `theorem_1_3_4.lean`. The final theorem reduces the
  average-degree hypothesis to a nonempty induced subgraph of minimum degree at
  least `2` using `proposition_1_2_2`, transfers the girth lower bound to that

  AHL branching factor `Λ`, proves `average_degree G - 1 ≤ Λ` when all degrees
  are at least `2`, and proves monotonicity of `n₀(d,g)` in `d` for `d ≥ 2`.
